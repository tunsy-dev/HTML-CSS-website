1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business.

IT has a vital role in modern business. From communications, advertising, global reach, effective algorithms, machine learning, and more. Any business that has embraced the innovation of technology will have an advantage in its competitive market than one that has not. Through IT businesses have access to data, software, and hardware that can drastically increase a business’s productivity and effectiveness in the market. With technology, for example, a shop can now sell products globally instead of in their immediate vicinity, with this increase of potential consumers, there is also an increase in competitors. This results in a high value being put on the online aspect of a business. Online sales can be seen to be a much larger revenue for many businesses [1]. This also results in higher emphasis being put on digital marketing as it can now be seen to be more efficient than newspaper television and radio advertising [2]. Technology has also given companies the ability to use consumer data to target ads and products. Showing just how important IT has become in this aspect of modern business.

IT has also massively bolstered the productivity of teams and businesses by providing them with tools through differing software to complete tasks much more effectively than they would have been able to without them. For example, the Software on your computer can check for spelling and grammar mistakes, reducing the time it would take to check a piece of work. And there are now endless amounts of software to help with almost every task you or your business might come across.

The importance of IT’s role in communication and every aspect of the business has been highlighted during the coronavirus pandemic. Through technology employees and businesses have been able to carry on functioning. Cloud networks have become a much more efficient method to store data and monitor a computer network than a private computer network. They also allow employees access to data and information from anywhere. Meaning that they can share work and carry on collaborating on projects. Software such as zoom and google hangouts has stepped in to replace physical communication. Allowing direct communication between employees and meetings to still happen.

“IT has continued its relentless march into almost every aspect of organizational life, and as innovation itself has taken on greater importance as a driver of organizational competitiveness.” (Hamel, 1998) [3].

A business that has embraced technology in their innovation will be seen to have more accurate business planning, more effective marketing, higher global sales, more systematic management, use real-time monitoring, and offer instant customer support. making them much more competitive in their market [4]. It is clear to see the importance of It in so many aspects of a business. Technologies are developed to make business more efficient in improving productivity and reach. However, with new technologies being developed and the speed of this, it is essential that businesses adapt, make use, and implement new technologies where appropriate. In order to carry on competing in a competitive market if not they can very easily find themselves behind the curve and out of competition.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

There are multiple risks that might result in a digital project failing. Billions of dollars are wasted each year on failed software projects. IT experts agree that these are often predictable and avoidable software however failure occurs far more often than they should [1].

So why do so many digital projects fail?
Often failure can be due to inadequate planning. A Report in 2016 by Innotas found that 55 percent of IT professionals surveyed have had a project fail due to lack of time, staff resources, or budget [2]. There are so many things that can go wrong when it comes to planning a project and many companies often have unrealistic expectations of how long a project will take or cost that results in the project failing [2]. Unclear project requirements can also contribute to a project failing. It could, unfortunately, be that the project brief wasn’t clear enough resulting in developers being unsure of what features are needed in the project. Or the end result of a project if the company intends to take it in a different direction or scale it up after completion [2]. If there is a lack of alignment in a team it is likely to cause a digital project to fail. If there is a lack of communication or understanding across a team tasks will get missed out, deadlines will get missed and there will be greater errors when working on a digital project, for this reason, it is vital to have good communication in a team [3]. On top of these, a lack of testing can cause a project to fail. This could be caused by poor planning and time management If time management hasn’t been apt testing could be sacrificed. This may result in the software being released with errors and bugs in it. If this happens consumers may be sceptical about your product and are likely to go elsewhere. Loss of data could also impact a project causing it to fail. If a company loses the personal data of consumers. Or if the data needed to implement a digital project is lost a project could very easily fail. However, due to cloud network storage, this is unlikely. Human Error is always a risk on all types of projects. Through software and planning, we can limit the risk of human error impacting a project. For example, cloud servers limit the risk of someone losing the data for a project.

The aforementioned risks can be limited with proper planning however there are some risks that can implement a digital project that cannot be avoided and are out of the direct control of the developer. olitical and global issues can affect your digital project for example if hardware necessary to complete your digital project becomes difficult to access you may have to cancel your project. An example of this is when the US imposed sanctions on China’s Huawei banning sales of chips made with American equipment to Huawei. And google stopped the phones being able to use their android OS in future models [4]. Changes in the market can cause a digital project to fail. If the time taken to implement a project has been too long or unfortunate there could no longer be a market for the project you are working on. This could also be the case if a competitor brings out a similar product before the completion of your project. Which would ultimately reduce the size of your target market.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

On May 25, 2018, the new European privacy regulation came into effect taking on the acronym GDPR. General Data Protection Regulation. The GDPR is a regulation in EU law on data protection and privacy in the European Union and the European Economic Area. It also addresses the transfer of personal data outside the EU and EEA areas [1]. It’s the EU’s way to give individuals more rights over there data and take power away from organizations using personal data for monetary gain [2].

Under the GDPR, individuals have:

  • The right to access.
  • The right to be forgotten.
  • The right to data portability.
  • The right to be informed.
  • The right to have the information corrected.
  • The right to restrict processing.
  • The right to object.
  • The right to be notified.

Since the implementation of the GDPR when it comes to digital projects, we have to be more thoughtful about what we are developing. Making sure that we are more transparent about how data is collected and used. It is important to be more considerate of the user. And also, necessary to be more thorough in your development and documentation. [3]
